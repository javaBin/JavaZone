JavaZone 2015 is well on its way!

Each year a group of volunteers called javaBin comes together to create a different conference, something uniquely norwegian. This year is no dofferent.

We invite more than 150 speakers to enlighten us for three full days. The first day is dedicated to workshops, the two next have presentations and lightning talks, as well as workshops.

First things first

The first thing you need is a ticket. Since we believe in simplicity, our tickets are also quite simple: the JavaZone ticket gives you full access access to the whole conference – including the workshops (registrations on a first come, first served basis). If you are a member of javaBin, you even get a discount.

Get your ticket now

Are you a student? You can join JavaZone Academy for free, and get a taste of what JavaZone is all about. Register today.

A few practicalities

Location, location, location..

As always, JavaZone takes place at Oslo Spektrum in the heart of Oslo. If this doesn't tell you anything – don't worry, finding Oslo Spektrum won't be too hard. You can use use this link to get directions from your current location to Oslo Spektrum, or use the travel planner with Oslo Spektrum as your destination for public transport. Ohh, and if you're completely lost, don't be afraid to ask! Most norwegians are quite friendly.

Food and drinks

Learning makes you hungry, and we are of course well aware of this. This is why we have a constant stream of food throughout the whole day, prepared by professional chefs from [insert chefs here!]. Believe us, if there is one thing you will not be missing at JavaZone, it is food.

Where do I stay?

There's plenty of hotels in the area surrounding the conference center. Searching for hotels close to "Oslo Central Station" is a good tip, and should give you a few alternatives to choose from.

I don't speak norwegian..

Don't worry! About 50% of the talks are typically held in english, and the spoken language is always specified in the program. You should still get plenty of learning out of the conference.

Formats & Topics

JavaZone has a few different presentation formats – full presentations, lightning talks and workshops. This means that you should have something nice to attend both when you just want a little teaser, and when you're in for the full package.

The full program will be available later, but you can have a sneak peak at the topics already now.

  • Architecture
    Architecture in Practice, Application Architecture, Designs, Scalability
  • Backend
    Data, Integration, Server, Cloud, NoSQL
  • Theory
    Features in Languages, Paradigms (i.e. Functional, Reactive)
  • Craftsmanship
    Clean Code, Continuous Delivery, DevOps
  • Frontend
    UX, Apps, Mobile, Responsive, HTML5, JavaFX
  • Java Platforms
    Java SE, Java EE, Java ME, Core, Memory, APIs, Performance, JVM Languages, Memory, 20 years of Java
  • Cross Cutting Concerns
    Security, Internet of Things, Big Data, JavaScript
